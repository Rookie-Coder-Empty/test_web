My Favorite Works with Critical Analyses

The Road Not Taken

By Robert Frost, 1916

Two roads diverged in a yellow wood,
And sorry I could not travel both
And be one traveler, long I stood
And looked down one as far as I could
To where it bent in the undergrowth;
...

[Full poem available at: Poetry Foundation]

Analysis

Robert Frost's "The Road Not Taken" is often misinterpreted as a celebration of individualism and nonconformity. However, a closer reading reveals that the poem is more about the inevitability of choice and the human tendency to retrospectively assign meaning to our decisions. The speaker encounters two paths that are "really about the same," suggesting that the choice is arbitrary. Yet, in the final stanza, the speaker imagines a future where they will claim that choosing the less traveled path "has made all the difference," highlighting how we often romanticize our past choices. This poem invites readers to reflect on the nature of decision-making and the narratives we construct about our lives.

Bohemian Rhapsody

By Queen, 1975

Is this the real life? Is this just fantasy?
Caught in a landslide, no escape from reality.
Open your eyes, look up to the skies and see,
I'm just a poor boy, I need no sympathy,
Because I'm easy come, easy go, little high, little low,
Any way the wind blows doesn't really matter to me, to me.
...

[Full lyrics available at: AZ Lyrics]

Analysis

"Bohemian Rhapsody" by Queen is a groundbreaking song that defies conventional song structures, blending elements of rock, opera, and ballad. Its six-minute duration and eclectic composition were unprecedented for a mainstream rock song in 1975. The lyrics are enigmatic and open to interpretation, with themes of existentialism, guilt, and redemption. Some speculate that the song reflects Freddie Mercury's personal struggles, while others see it as a fictional narrative. The song's enduring popularity can be attributed to its musical innovation, emotional depth, and the way it invites listeners to find their own meaning in its cryptic lyrics.

The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time

By Nintendo, 1998, Platform: Nintendo 64

"The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time" is an action-adventure game where players control Link, a young hero who must prevent the evil Ganondorf from obtaining the Triforce, a sacred relic that grants immense power. The game features a vast open world, intricate dungeons, and a time-travel mechanic that allows players to switch between Link's childhood and adulthood.

Analysis

Released in 1998, "Ocarina of Time" is widely regarded as one of the greatest video games of all time. It set new standards for 3D gameplay, storytelling, and world-building in video games. The game's innovative Z-targeting system revolutionized combat in 3D space, and its use of music as a gameplay mechanic added a unique dimension to puzzle-solving. The narrative, which explores themes of courage, friendship, and the passage of time, resonates deeply with players. Its influence can be seen in countless games that followed, cementing its legacy in gaming history.
